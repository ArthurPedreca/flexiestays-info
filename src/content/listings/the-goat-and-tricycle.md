The Goat and Tricycle is one of Bournemouth's most characterful traditional pubs, a short walk from the town centre and a firm favourite with those who take their beer seriously. Step inside and you'll find a cosy, unpretentious bar with a genuinely local feel and a warm welcome.

## Ales and atmosphere

The main draw is the range of cask real ales and ciders, with a regularly changing selection that keeps regulars coming back. It's a proper pub for conversation rather than loud music, and the sheltered courtyard is a suntrap on a fine afternoon. Pints of cask ale start from around £4.50.

## Where it is

The pub sits just back from the heart of town, an easy walk from the shops, the gardens and the seafront. After a session here you're only a short stroll from the promenade and a bite at [West Beach Restaurant](/listing/west-beach-restaurant).

## Good to know

Dogs are welcome throughout, so it's a good stop on a town-centre wander. Expect it to be busiest on weekend evenings, when the ale fans are out in force.
