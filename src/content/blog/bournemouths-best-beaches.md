Bournemouth's coastline is one long ribbon of soft, golden sand but each stretch has its own character. Here is how to choose the right one for your day, from west to east.

## Alum Chine Beach

Sheltered and family-focused, [Alum Chine](/listing/alum-chine-beach) is known for its tropical-themed gardens tumbling down to the sea and a well-loved adventure playground just behind the sand.

## Durley Chine Beach

A little closer to the centre, [Durley Chine](/listing/durley-chine-beach) is a relaxed, spacious beach with beach huts, watersports and easy seafront parking.

## Bournemouth Beach

The main event. [Bournemouth Beach](/listing/bournemouth-beach) sits either side of the pier, with the fullest range of cafés, deckchairs and facilities and the liveliest atmosphere in summer.

## Boscombe Beach

Home to the restored pier and a surf reef, [Boscombe Beach](/listing/boscombe-beach) has a creative, laid-back feel and some of the best independent cafés on the front.

## Southbourne Beach

Quieter and more local, [Southbourne Beach](/listing/southbourne-beach) offers dog-friendly sands and a gentle clifftop backdrop away from the crowds.

## Before you go

Most of these beaches hold Blue Flag and Seaside Awards for water quality and cleanliness. Check tide times, pack sun cream for our sunny microclimate, and see [what's on](/whats-on) at the seafront during your visit.
